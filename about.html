About Tiger Martial Arts

Discover our story, mission, and commitment to helping beginners transform their lives through martial arts.

Our Story

Founded in 2010, Iron Will Dojo began with a simple vision: create a martial arts school where beginners feel welcome, supported, and empowered to discover their potential.

Our founder, Master Sarah Chen, recognized that many martial arts schools were intimidating for newcomers. She wanted to create a different kind of dojo - one where technique, character, and personal growth mattered more than ego or competition.

Today, we've helped over 2,000 students begin their martial arts journey, from complete beginners to confident practitioners. Our approach remains the same: patient instruction, supportive community, and a focus on personal development.

Our Mission & Values

Everything we do is guided by our core mission to make martial arts accessible, enjoyable, and transformative for beginners.

Beginner-First Approach

We design every class, program, and interaction with beginners in mind. No intimidation, no pressure - just supportive guidance at your own pace.

Community Over Competition

While we respect martial arts traditions, we prioritize building a supportive community where students help each other grow and succeed.

Personal Growth Focus

Beyond physical techniques, we emphasize character development, confidence building, and life skills that extend far beyond the dojo.

Our Teaching Philosophy

Respect • Discipline • Growth • Community

We believe martial arts should build character first, technique second. Our approach emphasizes respect for others, self-discipline, continuous improvement, and mutual support among students.

Every student progresses at their own pace. We celebrate small victories, encourage effort over perfection, and create an environment where making mistakes is part of the learning process.

Traditional Values

  • • Respect for instructors and peers
  • • Discipline in training and life
  • • Humility and continuous learning

Modern Approach
